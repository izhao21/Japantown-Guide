Japantown Guide

“Top 3 Activities in San Francisco Japantown”

  • Kabuki Theatre
  • Benihana
  • Pagoda

Kabuki Theatre

Kabuki Theatre in Japantown, San Francisco, is a historic cultural venue that reflects the area's rich Japanese heritage. Originally opened in 1960 as a movie theater, it has evolved into a hub for showcasing Japanese films, community events, and cultural programming. Located within the Japan Center, it offers a blend of modern entertainment with a nod to traditional Japanese arts. While it no longer hosts live Kabuki performances, its name honors the iconic Japanese theatrical tradition, and the venue remains a beloved part of Japantown's vibrant community.

Benihana

Benihana is a popular Japanese teppanyaki restaurant chain known for its entertaining dining experience, where skilled chefs prepare meals on a flat grill right in front of guests. Founded in 1964 by Hiroaki "Rocky" Aoki, the restaurant combines delicious Japanese-inspired cuisine with theatrical cooking, including knife tricks and interactive chef performances. Benihana is famous for dishes like hibachi-grilled steak, seafood, chicken, fried rice, and signature dipping sauces. With its unique blend of dining and entertainment, Benihana has become a global brand and a favorite spot for celebrations and group outings.

Pagoda

The Peace Pagoda in San Francisco's Japantown is an iconic, five-tiered monument symbolizing peace and friendship. It was a gift from Osaka, Japan, one of San Francisco's sister cities, and was designed by Japanese architect Yoshiro Taniguchi. Built in 1968, the pagoda stands in the Japan Center's Peace Plaza and serves as a cultural and spiritual centerpiece for the community. The structure hosts various festivals and events, including cherry blossom celebrations, and represents the enduring bond between Japan and San Francisco, as well as a broader message of global harmony.
